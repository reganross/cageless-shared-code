Last updated: `2026-03-30`

This document describes the *creative intent* behind **Cageless** so art, systems, and content decisions stay consistent over time.

## 1. Art Style

### Target feel

- *(Draft)* Stylized-but-grounded world: clearly readable gameplay space, with enough detail to feel alive.
- *(Draft)* Economy-focused visual language: materials, crafting, and transactions should be visually emphasized.
- *(Draft)* Social visibility: players should be recognizable in crowds (silhouettes, simple costume kits, readable nameplates).

### Visual direction

1. World: *(choose one)* semi-realistic / low-poly stylized / hand-painted / voxel-like

## 2. Gameplay Loops

### Design goal

Make the MMORPG feel like **skill + economy + social momentum**, not “grind for hours” labor.

### Core loop (highest-level)

1. Arrive in the world and choose a short objective aligned to your role (gatherer / crafter / trader / organizer).
2. Participate in gathering or production activities to earn usable materials and crafting currency.
3. Convert that output into value through crafting and/or market transactions.
4. Engage with competition that rewards smart participation (for example: contests, market influence, or economy-driven challenges).
5. End the session with inventory decisions that make the next play session easier.

### Supporting sub-loops (examples we can build first)

- Exploration & sourcing: find resource nodes, routes, and opportunities that fit your build.
- Crafting & specialization: crafting recipes, skill progression, and meaningful output variation.
- Trading & marketplace play: pricing strategy, supply/demand incentives, and reputation.
- Community & events: guilds/parties organizing gatherings, contests, and “economy days”.
- Skill progression without burnout: progress should come from decision-making and contribution, not time spent idle.

### Progression principles

- Progress should be “layered”: short wins + long-term goals.
- Reputation and economy standing should unlock playstyles rather than mandatory gear treadmills.
- Competition should be readable and fair (even if players can outsmart each other).

### Open choices

- How “competition” works in practice: contests / auctions / economy influence / crafting tournaments
- Whether there are combat stats at all (or if combat is limited/secondary)

## 3. Average 20-Minute Play Session Loop

This is the target *typical* experience a new or returning player can complete in about **20 minutes**.

### Timeline (draft target)

1. 0:00–3:00 — Login + choose intention
   - Quick menu decision: “Gather”, “Craft”, “Trade”, or “Compete”.
   - Receive a lightweight objective and suggested route.
2. 3:00–8:00 — Source inputs (gathering/exploration)
   - Gather materials or capture opportunities relevant to your objective.
   - Gain immediate feedback: what you found and how it affects your next steps.
3. 8:00–13:00 — Convert inputs into value (crafting/production)
   - Craft items or refine materials into outputs that have market utility.
   - Skill checks should feel like *choices*, not grinding.
4. 13:00–17:00 — Social/economic action (market or trading)
   - Price strategy, buy/sell, fulfill orders, or contribute to a community stock.
5. 17:00–20:00 — Competition contribution + session closure
   - Participate in an event window or submit an offering that influences outcomes.
   - Stash/plan: short summary screen of what improved and what to do next time.

### What “success” feels like

- You finish the session with tangible value: items, reputation, progress, and a clear next step.
- You participated socially or economically (even if you only sold/bought once).
- You didn’t need to commit to a long multi-hour run to feel progress.
