# Cageless Contributor Agreement (v1)

Last updated: `2026-03-30`

This document sets the agreed terms for contributors to the project titled **Cageless**.

> Not legal advice. If you want this to be enforceable as a formal contract in your jurisdiction, consult a lawyer.

---

## 1. Definitions

1. **Project**: the game titled **Cageless**.
2. **Contributor**: a person who has agreed to this document and is accepted as a contributor by the process in Section 3.
3. **Existing contributors**: all contributors who are currently accepted at the time a vote is taken.
4. **Meeting attendance**: attending at least one scheduled project meeting within the applicable period.
5. **Code contribution**: contributing production-relevant code to the project repository (for example via pull request/merge) within the applicable period.

---

## 2. Ownership and sharing

1. **Equal sharing of ownership**: Each contributor has an equal ownership interest in the project.
2. Ownership shares are intended to be equal among all current contributors as defined in Section 1.

---

## 3. Adding new contributors

1. New contributors may be added only by a vote of **existing contributors**.
2. A new contributor is accepted if **75% or higher** agreement is reached between the existing contributors.
3. The vote result must be recorded (for example in the repository discussions/issues or a meeting note) with:
   1. proposed contributor identity
   2. date of vote
   3. list of voters and the outcome

---

## 4. Amending this document

1. Amendments to this document require a vote of **current contributors**.
2. An amendment is approved if **75% of current contributors** vote in favor.
3. The amendment must be recorded with the updated document version and effective date.

---

## 5. Contributor removal for non-participation

1. If a contributor:
   1. does not attend meetings for **3 months** (assuming meetings are being held), and
   2. does not contribute production-relevant code for **3 months**,
   then the contributor may be removed.
2. Removal requires a vote by **all other contributors** (excluding the contributor being considered).
3. The removal is approved if **75% or higher** of the other contributors vote in favor.
4. If the contributor removal vote passes, their contributor status is revoked starting from the vote date (or a mutually agreed effective date).

---

## 6. Creative decision authority

Regan has the final say on all creative decisions on the project titled **Cageless**.
